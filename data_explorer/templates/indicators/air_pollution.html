Health effects of air pollution

This tool estimates short-term health impacts of ambient PM2.5 on all-cause mortality using a distributed-lag generalised additive model with meta-analysis across regions. Attributable burden is computed against one or more reference standards (e.g. WHO 15 μg/m³).

Full details of the methodology are provided in the Air pollution framework documentation

Important information:

Using user input data, this calculator returns the following results:

  • Region-level and pooled relative risk of mortality across distributed lag windows.
  • Attributable fractions and numbers of deaths versus a chosen reference standard.
  • Optional descriptive statistics and power analysis for the input data.

Important information:

The default model estimates mortality attributable to ambient PM2.5 using a distributed-lag generalised additive model with a quasi-Poisson family and a cyclic seasonal spline, pooled across regions with a multivariate meta-analysis.

Full details of the methodology are provided in the Air pollution framework documentation

Default (baseline) model specification:

  • Required exposure variables: daily deaths, daily PM2.5, population, humidity, precipitation, maximum temperature and wind speed.
  • Maximum PM2.5 lag of 14 days, with seasonal smoothing controlled by a spline of 6 degrees of freedom.
  • Attributable burden is computed against the WHO short-term guideline value of 15 μg/m³ by default. Additional reference standards can be added in the advanced parameters as a JSON list.
